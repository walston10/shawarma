San Antonio's Best
Brazilian Shawarma.

Picanha turns slowly on the vertical rotisserie, fat rendering down the cone, the outer edge crisping to a deep mahogany. A cook shaves the crust into warm laffa, still blistered from the oven, and finishes the wrap with chimichurri whisked through tahini, mint torn by hand, pickled onions cut that morning. It's the wrap you'd line up for in São Paulo on a Tuesday afternoon — pulled together, plate to counter, in under four minutes. Now in San Antonio.
